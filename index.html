What Have I Done?

Did you think you were coming here to see a list of my confessions? Hilarious, but no.

I have worked a lot of different jobs through my adult years, and most of them have helped to shape who I am and where my path has led me. I like to share my job journey, so feel free to strike up a conversation with me!

I have worked in medical offices for most of my years: Family Practice and OB, Addiction Treatment, and Wound Care. I'll give you a diagnosis for half the price of your PCP!

I managed a retail "Romance Boutique" for several years. Close your ears if you're prone to embarrassment: it's a classy sex store. My favorite job to date, no question. Of course the job was fun, but I also loved helping so many people coming from different backgrounds and beliefs. I tell people I often got to practice being a sex therapist, just without a license. And unexpected bonus for me was that I was able to heal from some of my own past wounds.

I worked as an airline reservationist. As a newly single-mom, I thought this work-from-home job was the job of my dreams. *Wrong*

I was a literacy tutor for children with dyslexia and other different abilities. That job was deeply rewarding and so fascinating for me to learn things about my native language I had never known before. I was exhausted at the end of my shift as it was important to stay engaged every minute of each session, but my life was made better as I watched younger lives change for their future.

There have been other less memorable and/or desireable jobs, but I'm thankful for all of the learning and growth. Life is a long continuous path of learning. I love to learn about the human body, mind, and spirit. Health, relationships, communication, and how we process it all is always of interest to me.

Language 💬🌍🗣

I'm so intrigued by languages. Ever since I was a young lassie, I loved to spell and loved learning about grammar. My dad was always big on proper English and correct grammar, so maybe that's where I got it from, who knows?

Español ~ I took 3 years of Spanish in high school ... big deal, right? My maestra in Spanish class made us all choose a Spanish name to be called all semester long from a short list in the textbook. I got stuck with "Mercedes". Now, don't get offended. I was 16 years old and way less comfortable in my own skin, and I just didn't want to choose a name that wasn't mine. 😂 Life was hard back then.

Continuing my Español journey ~ At 21 young years of age, I volunteered to go on a church mission. To my great surprise, I was sent to Córdoba, Argentina! Life-changing, my friends! One of the greatest experiences of my whole life was to learn to serve and love people while learning to be fluent in Spanish. After just 7 months living in Argentina, I was complimented more than once by native Spanish-speakers that I sounded like one of them! That made me feel so good. When I returned home to the USA, I swore I would never "lose" my Spanish-speaking abilities. Well, it turns out that 25 years and a lot of not practicing a language will get you what you don't want. *Sigh*

ASL ~ The great American Sign Language! As much as I love Spanish, I also love ASL and believe every human being should know some basic sign language. I was exposed as a child to ASL, mostly by my mom. I'm not positive when or why my mom started learning ASL, but I remember being taught many signs in my youth. When I got big and strong and moved away to college, I enrolled in a beginning ASL class. One of my favorites! And not just because of the 1 cute boy in my class that ended up being my boyfriend.

Since the beginning of this year, I made a goal to practice and learn ASL better by using a cool phone app, which has been going great until the last 3 weeks. Don't worry - I'll get back up and start again!
